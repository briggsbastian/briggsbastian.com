A blog is a performance: posts are finished, dated, and slowly embarrassing.
A garden is a workspace: notes are planted small, tended when I learn
something, and allowed to stay unfinished forever.

## The rules I tend by

1. **Plant early.** A note earns its place by being a real question, not a
   finished answer. Most of what's here would be too half-formed to "publish."
2. **Tend in public.** When a note changes, it changes in place. The
   `tended` date tells you how alive it is; there's no pretense that the
   first version was right.
3. **Stages, not dates, signal trust.** ○ seedlings are barely more than a
   captured thought. ◐ budding notes have some structure and some conviction.
   ● evergreens are as settled as I get — which is still not very.

## Why bother?

Because the alternative is that thinking happens in terminal scrollback and
gets garbage-collected. The garden is where ideas from the
[projects](/projects/) go to be examined before they harden into code —
and where you can watch me change my mind, which is the most honest thing
a personal site can show.
